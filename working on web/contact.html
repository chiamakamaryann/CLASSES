Differences between Batch Distillation and Continous Distillation

Batch process Continous process
1 Separation is batch wise Separationis a continous process
2 It requires one distillation column for separation of the components on the mixture. It ensures n-1 column where n=the number of components needed to be separated.
3 The flexibility in batch distillation is very high The flexibilityis less because there are several distillation column used for each component separated from the mixture
4 The effeciency of the batch distillation is low The effeciency is high
5 The column can be used for different componentmixture quickly and efficiently It takes a long time to change the mixture that is being distilled.

The distillation techniquesare mostly used in the industry for proper separation of raw crude oil. Distillation is a proces of separation by vapourisation, a liquid mixture miscible and volatie substances into individual components or group of components eg, crude petroleum.

In this process, a solute is removed from either a liquid or gas through contact with a solid absorbent eg; ngos dehydration using solid absorbents that can be regenrated or reactivated eg; silica gel and silica alumina bits.

when a solid is transfered from the soluent to the gas phase, the operation is known as stripping. it is when volatile compoent is transfered into a gas.
